> **Document Status:** Living document — updated as research and design evolve
> **Last Updated:** 2026-02-17

## 1. The Metin2 Legacy

Metin2 (2004, Ymir Entertainment/Webzen) was a Korean MMORPG that found massive success in European and Turkish markets. It became a cultural phenomenon — particularly in Germany, Poland, Turkey, Romania, and Italy — not because it was the most polished MMO, but because it was **accessible, social, and addictive**.

### What Made Metin2 Special

1. **Low barrier to entry** — Ran on modest hardware, free-to-play model
2. **Kingdom War system** — Three factions (Shinsoo, Chunjo, Jinno) competing for territory created real stakes
3. **Metin Stones** — Destructible world objects that spawned monsters, gave loot, and required cooperation
4. **Equipment upgrade gambling** — The +1 to +9 (and beyond) system created thrilling risk/reward moments
5. **Fast-paced action combat** — Not tab-targeting like WoW, but real-time clicking with positioning mattering
6. **Horse/mount system** — One of the first F2P MMOs with rideable mounts and mounted combat
7. **Simple but deep class system** — 4 base classes with 2 specializations each, plus later Lycan
8. **Nostalgia-driven community** — Many players have deep emotional connections from childhood

### What Metin2 Got Wrong

1. **Pay-to-win escalation** — Item Mall items became increasingly necessary for competitive play
2. **Bot epidemic** — Automated farming destroyed the economy and new player experience
3. **Power creep** — Constant level cap and gear tier increases devalued older content
4. **Outdated graphics** — Engine from 2004 never received meaningful visual upgrades
5. **Poor anti-cheat** — Speedhacks, damage hacks, and duplication bugs were rampant
6. **Lack of meaningful endgame** — Beyond grinding levels and upgrading gear, little structured content
7. **Zone design** — Maps were often empty expanses with monster spawn fields, lacking environmental storytelling
8. **Quest system** — Mostly "kill X", "collect Y" fetch quests with no narrative depth

## 2. Why "Metin3" Never Happened

Webzen, the publisher, never committed to a true sequel for several reasons:

- **Private server fragmentation** — The leaked source code led to thousands of private servers, fragmenting the playerbase
- **Revenue from existing game** — Metin2 continued generating revenue through its Item Mall
- **Market shift** — The MMORPG market declined as MOBAs and battle royales took over
- **Development risk** — Building an MMO sequel is extremely expensive with uncertain ROI
- **Community expectations** — Any "Metin3" would face impossible nostalgia standards

### What the Community Wanted from a Sequel

Based on community forums, Reddit discussions, and private server trends:

1. **Modern graphics** with the same artistic identity (Eastern fantasy/martial arts)
2. **Fair monetization** — Cosmetic-only cash shop, no P2W
3. **Anti-cheat from day one** — Server-authoritative architecture
4. **Better combat** — More skill expression, combos, dodging, not just stat-checking
5. **Meaningful PvP** — Balanced kingdom wars, arena rankings, siege warfare
6. **Social features** — Better guild tools, housing, social spaces
7. **Content depth** — Dungeons with actual mechanics, storyline quests, achievements
8. **Mobile/browser accessibility** — Play anywhere without downloading a 10GB client
9. **Modern quality-of-life** — Better UI, inventory management, auto-pathing
10. **Economic balance** — Anti-bot measures, trade controls, inflation prevention

## 3. The Massless Vision

Massless is not a Metin2 clone — it's **what Metin3 should have been**, built for the browser era.

### Elevator Pitch

> **Massless** is a browser-based MMORPG that captures the fast-paced combat, kingdom rivalry, and addictive progression of Metin2, rebuilt with modern web technology. No downloads, no installs — just open your browser and enter a world where three kingdoms clash for dominance, Metin Stones corrupt the land, and your +9 sword is one upgrade attempt away from greatness or destruction.

### Core Design Pillars

#### Pillar 1: Instant Access
No client download. No launcher. Click a link, enter the world. WebGPU delivers AAA-adjacent visuals directly in the browser. Cross-platform by default — desktop, laptop, tablet.

#### Pillar 2: Skill-Based Combat
Combat rewards player skill — positioning, timing, combo execution, and tactical decisions — not just stat checks. Every class has depth. Auto-attack exists but is the floor, not the ceiling.

#### Pillar 3: Meaningful Rivalry
Three kingdoms aren't just labels — they define your experience. Territory control affects resource access. Kingdom wars have real consequences. Your guild's reputation matters.

#### Pillar 4: Risk/Reward Progression
The thrill of upgrading equipment, the tension of gambling on that +8 attempt, the satisfaction of a successful enchantment. Progression should feel exciting, not like a spreadsheet.

#### Pillar 5: Server Authority & Fairness
The server is the single source of truth. No speedhacks, no damage hacks, no duplication. Fair play is enforced by architecture, not by ban waves.

### What We Keep from Metin2

| Feature | Adaptation |
|---------|------------|
| Three Kingdoms (Shinsoo/Chunjo/Jinno) | Renamed but same faction triangle concept |
| Metin Stones | World event system — corrupted stones spawn, require group effort |
| Equipment upgrading (+1 to +9) | Keep the core loop, add protection/safety mechanics |
| Character classes | 4 base classes inspired by M2 archetypes, modernized skill trees |
| Mounted combat | Mount system with progression, mounted skills |
| Open-world PvP | Zone-based PvP rules (safe/contested/war zones) |
| Yang economy | Single primary currency with sink/faucet balance |
| Party play | Group bonuses, party dungeons, shared quests |
| Guild system | Guilds with territory, ranks, wars, guild dungeons |

### What We Improve

| Metin2 Problem | Massless Solution |
|----------------|-------------------|
| Pay-to-win | Cosmetic-only monetization, battle pass for aesthetics |
| Bots | Server-authoritative, behavioral detection, CAPTCHA gates |
| Empty maps | Zones with environmental storytelling, dynamic events, points of interest |
| Shallow quests | Quest chains with branching dialogue, zone storylines |
| Stat-check combat | Action combat with dodging, combos, skill shots, positioning |
| No endgame | Dungeons with mechanics, weekly raids, seasonal content, ranked PvP |
| Poor balance | Data-driven balancing, regular patches, PTR/test realm |
| Outdated engine | WebGPU rendering, modern animations, particle effects |

### What We Add (New to Massless)

1. **Dynamic Event System** — World bosses, invasions, seasonal events that transform zones
2. **Achievement System** — Titles, cosmetic rewards, long-term goals beyond leveling
3. **Crafting Depth** — Recipe discovery, material grades, specialization paths
4. **Housing/Camp System** — Personal space in the world, functional decorations
5. **Ranked Arena** — Structured PvP with matchmaking, seasons, leaderboards
6. **Dungeon Finder** — Cross-zone party matching for instanced content
7. **Mentoring System** — High-level players help newcomers for mutual rewards
8. **Transmog/Appearance** — Separate gear stats from appearance
9. **Battle Pass** — Seasonal progression track with cosmetic rewards

## 4. Target Audience

### Primary: Metin2 Veterans (25-35 years old)
- Played Metin2 as teenagers in 2006-2012
- Now have jobs, less time, but crave that nostalgic gameplay
- Want accessibility (browser) and fairness (no P2W)
- Active in Metin2 private server communities

### Secondary: MMORPG Enthusiasts
- Players looking for a new MMO that isn't a WoW clone
- Appreciate action combat and PvP focus
- Value low commitment (browser-based, no huge download)

### Tertiary: Browser Game Players
- Players who enjoy browser-based games
- Casual to mid-core, looking for depth in a browser title
- Surprised and delighted by the quality possible in modern browsers

## 6. Revenue Model (Future Consideration)

### Ethical Monetization Principles
1. **Never sell power** — No stat boosts, no P2W gear, no advantage items
2. **Cosmetics are king** — Skins, mounts, pets, effects, emotes
3. **Respect time** — XP boosts only in non-competitive contexts, if at all
4. **Transparent odds** — Any RNG-based cosmetic system shows exact probabilities

### Potential Revenue Streams
- Cosmetic shop (character skins, weapon effects, mount skins)
- Battle pass (seasonal cosmetic progression)
- Premium housing decorations
- Name change / appearance change tokens
- Server transfer tokens (if applicable)

> **Note:** Revenue model is Phase 4+. Focus first on building a game worth paying for.
